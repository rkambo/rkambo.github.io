When I worked as a Backend Intern a few years ago, I remember when a friend of mine was stuck on a problem that was plaguing him for a couple of hours. I remember taking a look at it and I said "Hey, what if you tried this?". His response was, "Wow that's so dumb, but..it worked? But it's so dumb!" And so on. We laughed for a couple of hours then too. It was moments like these that really inspired me to become a better Software Engineer. I learned that it wasn't always about applying logic to a problem, but you could approach things from a different angle, and sometimes get really creative. We're fortunate in the world of development to be able to fail quickly and safely; not just so that we can guarantee the right answer, but for the opportunity to get really imaginative.

Even in my current role, it's those developers who laughed the most that really inspired me to learn more. I've been taught by seniors who aren't afraid to give you those "dumb ideas", but can back them up with strong fundamentals and their depth of knowledge. More importantly, it taught me that being a true mentor isn't just about having that knowledge, but about the responsibility of inspiring growth and being a role model to strive for.
